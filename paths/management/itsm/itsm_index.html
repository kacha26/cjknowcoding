ITSM - Information Technology Service Management

The 4 pillers of ITSM:

  1. Users: Employees and customers that use IT services
  2. Services: Applications, hardware and infrastructure provided by IT team
  3. Quality: Effectiveness and efficiency
  4. Cost: Getting the most of an IT budget

ITSM Core Processes:

  1. Incident management:

    Incident: An incident is considered to be any event that could lead to a unplanned interruption of IT services or reduction in quality for the same.

    The objective of incident management is to restore a normal service of operation (in accordance with SLA) as soon as possible and to minimize the impact on the business operations. Thus ensuring the best possible levels of service quality and availability are maintained.

    Major Incident Management: It is an incident that results in significant disruption to the business and demands a response beyond the routine incident management process. Major incidents have a seperate procedure with smaller timescales and urgency that is required to accelerate the resolution process for incidents with high business impact. It is an incident with high priority that impacts multiple users, fincancial departments, critical systems, production plans etc.

    Incident life cycle:

    1. End user will report in an incident through email/chat/call.
    2. Help desk raises the incident in ITSM tool based on the end user's input.
    3. Help desk captures the crucial information related to the incident such as impact, urgency and location.
    4. Help desk will search for appropriate knowledge article (KA) and assign the incident to the relevant assignment group.
    5. Assignee will trouble shoot the incident by following the KA.
    6. Once addressed, the assignee, will change the state of the incident to resolved and fill in the resolution notes.
    7. If the end user does not confirm the resolution, he can reopen and the flow moves to step 5. If end user doesn't respond, the incident will be automatically closed in a predefined timeframe.

      8. Note: Incidents in resolved state can be reopened but not the incidents in closed state.

    Major incident life cycle:

    1. End user will report the incident through email/chat/call.
    2. Help desk raises the incident in ITSM tool based on the end user input.
    3. Help desk realises it is an major incident (MI) and reports to Major Incident Manager (MIM).
    4. MIM realises it is not suitable for MI and follow regular IM process. If found suitable, the MIM will raise the priority and assign the MI to the appropriate group.
    5. Post assignment, the MIM will send out communication to both internal and to the customers and if needed he will create a bridge call with the teams that are working on the MI.
    6. The MIM will constantly follow up with the involved teams and keep the stakeholders informaed through out the MI life cycle.
    7. Post resolution confirmation, the MI will be marked as resolved. It will be reported to problem manager to perform root cause analysis (RCA) to contribute to CSI initiative.

  2. Request fulfillment:

    The Request fulfillment is the process responcible for managing the life cycle of all service requests from the users. These service requests can vary from an access, a new laptop or a software installation.

    Not all organizations have this process in place, however, if number of service requests is a large one, it would be smart to handle service requests as a seperate work stream. Examples: password reset, access to a shared folder, installing an unauthorised software etc.

    Request fulfillment life cycle:

    1. End user initiates the service request via service now
      • The main ticket itself is called a request
      • What the user is asking for is called a requested item
      • What the agents and suppliers are doing to fulfill the request are called tasks
    2. Depending on whether the request need any approval or not, it moved to 3rd or 4th step
    3. Approvers approves the request
    4. Transverse team checks stock
    5. Transverse team orders and awaits the delivery of the product
    6. Transverse team configures the requested item
    7. Transverse team delivers the requested item and moves the state to completed / closed

  3. Problem management:

    A Problem is an unknown root cause of one or more existing and potential incidents. Problems may sometimes be identified because of multiple incidents that exhibit common symptoms

    Goals:

    • Minimize the adverse impact of the incidents and problems on the business, that is caused by errors within services and technology
    • Prevent the recurrence of incidents releated to same error through RCA of the incidents and initiate actions to correct or improve the situation

    Objectives:

    • Discover and remove errors from the infrastructure
    • To assure delivery of highest possible level of stability in IT services by finding and eliminating errors
